Halloween in Boston is a vibrant and festive time, with numerous events and activities happening throughout the city. Some of the highlights include:

Haunted Happenings in Salem: While not technically in Boston, Salem, Massachusetts, just north of the city, is renowned for its Halloween celebrations. Salem is famous for its witch trials of 1692, and during October, the city hosts "Haunted Happenings," a month-long festival featuring parades, haunted tours, costume balls, and other spooky events.

Halloween Parties and Events: Many bars, clubs, and venues in Boston host Halloween parties and events. These range from costume contests to themed parties with live music and entertainment.

Trick-or-Treating: Neighborhoods across Boston participate in trick-or-treating, with families and children dressing up in costumes and going door-to-door for candy. Some neighborhoods, like Beacon Hill, are particularly well-known for their elaborate Halloween decorations and festivities.

Haunted Tours and Ghost Walks: Boston is a city rich in history, and several companies offer haunted tours and ghost walks throughout the city. These tours often highlight spooky tales and historical events, adding an extra layer of excitement to Halloween celebrations.

Pumpkin Carving and Decorating: Many community centers, parks, and organizations in Boston host pumpkin carving and decorating events leading up to Halloween. These events are often family-friendly and provide an opportunity for creativity and community engagement.

Halloween Events at Museums and Attractions: Some museums and attractions in Boston offer special Halloween-themed events and exhibits. This can include spooky tours, haunted houses, and family-friendly activities centered around Halloween themes.

Costume Shops and Halloween Stores: Boston has several costume shops and Halloween stores where residents and visitors can purchase or rent costumes, accessories, decorations, and other Halloween-related items.
